Sensor synchronization

In order to achieve good cross-modality data alignment between 

the LIDAR and the cameras, 

the exposure of a camera is triggered when the top LIDAR sweeps across the center of the camera’s FOV. 

The timestamp of the image is the exposure trigger time; and the timestamp of the LIDAR scan is the time when the full rotation of the current LIDAR frame is achieved. 

Given that the camera’s exposure time is nearly instantaneous, this method generally yields good data alignment. 

Note that the cameras run at 12Hz while the LIDAR runs at 20Hz. 

The 12 camera exposures are spread as evenly as possible across the 20 LIDAR scans, so not all LIDAR scans have a corresponding camera frame. 

educing the frame rate of the cameras to 12Hz helps to reduce the compute, bandwidth and storage requirement of the perception system.
